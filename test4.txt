Correct answer

Continuous data can only take on integer values, whereas discrete data can take on any value, including decimals.

Overall explanation
In the context of data analysis, especially relevant for the Databricks Data Analyst Associate Certification Exam, it's crucial to understand the distinction between discrete and continuous data as they have different applications and implications in statistical analysis.



Discrete data are quantitative and can take only fixed, distinct values. These values are countable, but not necessarily whole numbers. For example, shoe sizes can be 7, 7.5, 8, etc., but you won't find a shoe size of 7.7. They are characterized by clear gaps between values and are generally less precise due to their distinct nature. Discrete data are often visualized using bar charts, pie charts, or scatter plots. Examples of discrete data include the number of students in a class, cars in a parking lot, or days in a month with temperatures above a certain degree.



On the other hand, continuous data can assume any value within a range and can be meaningfully divided into smaller parts, including fractional and decimal values. They represent measurements and can be infinitely precise, as they can potentially be measured to an ever-increasing degree of accuracy. Continuous data are typically represented using histograms or line charts, ideal for showcasing trends or patterns over time. Common examples include temperature, weight, height, and the time it takes for runners to complete a race.
